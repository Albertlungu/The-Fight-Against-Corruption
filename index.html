Corruption 101

Corruption: The abuse of power for personal gain.

Bribery: Offering something of value to influence actions.

Embezzlement: Theft of funds by someone trusted to manage them.

Nepotism: Favoring relatives or friends in jobs or positions of power.

SDG 16 in Real Life

  • Education: In the Democratic Republic of the Congo, corruption in teacher payroll systems delays salaries and reduces teaching quality.
  • Healthcare: In Nigeria, patients often must pay bribes to access urgent care, weakening trust in hospitals.
  • Housing: In Brazil, embezzlement in public housing programs leaves thousands without shelter.
  • Climate Action: Corruption in environmental permits leads to illegal deforestation and pollution globally.
  • Policing: Surveys show police bribery is one of the most reported forms of everyday corruption in Latin America.
